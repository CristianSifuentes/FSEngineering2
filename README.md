## Integrated Knowledge: Electronic Circuits

Electronic circuits form the foundation of all modern technology. Understanding how electricity flows through components and how those components interact allows us to bridge the gap between physical hardware and abstract software.

---

## Electricity and Current Flow

- Electricity is the **movement of electrons** through a conductive material.
- Conceptual flow direction: **negative pole ‚Üí positive pole** (water-flow analogy).
- **Voltage** is the electrical pressure that drives the current.
- **Amperage (current)** represents the quantity of electrons flowing through the circuit.
- Higher amperage ‚áí more available energy ‚áí faster charging *(if the device supports it)*.

---

## Power Sources

Electronic circuits can be powered by multiple sources:
- Electrical grid (power plants ‚Üí homes).
- Batteries (portable stored energy).
- Alternative sources (solar panels, generators).

---

## Basic Components and Their Function

### Resistors
- Reduce voltage and current.
- Dissipate electrical energy as heat.
- Measured in **ohms (Œ©)**.
- Essential for protecting sensitive components.

### LEDs
- Convert electrical energy into light.
- Require resistors to prevent overcurrent damage.

### Electric Motors
- Convert electrical energy into mechanical motion using magnetic fields.
- Common types:
  - Standard motors (home appliances)
  - Servo motors (robotics and precision systems)
  - High-power motors (electric vehicles)

### Speakers / Loudspeakers
- Convert electrical signals into sound.
- An electrical waveform causes a membrane to vibrate.

---

## Logical and Control Components

### Switches
- Allow or interrupt the flow of electricity.
- Basis of the **on / off** concept.

### Thermistors
- Change electrical resistance based on temperature.
- Fundamental for thermal sensors and thermostats.

### Transistors
- Automatic electronic switches.
- Control current flow based on specific conditions.
- Form the **foundation of modern computing**.

---

## Relationship with Modern Computing

### Processors
- A microchip is composed of **millions or billions of transistors**.
- These transistors perform mathematical and logical operations at extremely high speeds.

### Memory and Storage

**Capacitors**
- Temporarily store electrical charge.
- Charged = `1`, Discharged = `0`.

**RAM**
- Built from groups of capacitors.
- Volatile: data is lost when power is removed.

**Hard Disk Drives (HDD)**
- Permanent magnetic storage.

**SSD / USB Storage**
- Solid-state electronic storage with no moving parts.

## From Hardware to Software

- Program code exists as **bits stored in memory**.
- These bits travel through circuits to the processor.
- The CPU interprets them as **real executable instructions**.
- This process gives rise to:
  - Applications
  - Operating systems
  - Graphical user interfaces
  - Audio, video, and networking systems
